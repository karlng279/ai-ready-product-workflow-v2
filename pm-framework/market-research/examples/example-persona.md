# Example: Persona — Sarah Chen, Senior Operations Manager

*This is a worked example following the persona-template. It shows a Jobs-aware persona grounded in customer interview data.*

---

## Profile

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Name** | Sarah Chen |
| **Role / Title** | Senior Operations Manager |
| **Industry** | Freight forwarding — mid-size |
| **Company size** | 220 employees, 3 offices (Singapore HQ, Malaysia, Thailand) |
| **Experience in role** | 8 years in operations; 3 years at current company |
| **Location** | Singapore |
| **Tech comfort** | Medium — comfortable with SaaS tools, not a technical user |

---

## Jobs-to-be-Done

### Functional Job (Primary)

> "Track the real-time status of all my active shipments — across all carriers and lanes — without having to log into each carrier portal or call anyone."

### Functional Job (Secondary)

> "Know immediately when a shipment is delayed so I can proactively notify the customer before they call me."

### Emotional Job

> Feel in control and confident at all times — even when the shipping situation is chaotic. Never be caught flat-footed by a question she should know the answer to.

### Social Job

> Be known as the person on the team who always has answers and keeps operations running smoothly. Her manager calls her "the most reliable person in the office."

---

## Current Solution

**What she uses today:** A shared Google Sheet (updated manually twice a day) + individual carrier portals (Maersk, MSC, OOCL, CMA CGM) + WhatsApp groups with her forwarders.

**Why she settled for it:** "It's what we've always done. The carriers don't talk to each other and there's no single platform that covers all of them."

**What she likes about it:** The spreadsheet gives her team a shared record. WhatsApp is fast for urgent updates.

---

## Pains

| # | Pain | Severity |
|---|------|----------|
| 1 | Manually updating the tracking spreadsheet takes 2 hours per day across her team | High |
| 2 | Carrier portals each have different UX; she has to learn 5 different systems | Medium |
| 3 | Delays are discovered reactively — when the customer calls, not before | High |
| 4 | The shared spreadsheet is often out of date; team members forget to update it | High |
| 5 | No audit trail — when something goes wrong, it's hard to reconstruct what happened | Medium |

---

## Gains

| # | Gain | Importance |
|---|------|------------|
| 1 | Answer any customer status question in under 30 seconds | Essential |
| 2 | Know about delays before the customer does | Essential |
| 3 | Eliminate the daily spreadsheet update routine | Nice-to-have |
| 4 | Have a historical log of all shipment events for dispute resolution | Unexpected delight |

---

## Switching Trigger

> A key customer — a major electronics retailer — called angry because a shipment was delayed by 4 days and Sarah's team had no idea. The customer threatened to move to a competitor. That evening, Sarah Googled "shipment tracking software for freight forwarders."

**Trigger type:** ☑ Pain threshold reached

---

## Buying Behavior (B2B)

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Decision-making role** | Champion (evaluates and recommends) + day-to-day user |
| **Budget authority** | Needs COO approval for contracts > SGD 15,000/year |
| **Evaluation criteria** | 1. Carrier coverage (must include her top 5) 2. Ease of use (her team isn't technical) 3. Price (has a limited ops budget) |
| **Preferred buying channel** | Free trial — she needs to see it work with her actual shipments before recommending it |
| **Evaluation timeline** | 3–4 week trial, then a decision |

---

## Key Quotes

> "I spend the first 2 hours of every morning just updating a spreadsheet. That's not operations — that's data entry."

> "The worst feeling is when a customer calls and I have to say 'let me check and call you back.' That should never happen."

> "I don't need fancy analytics or AI predictions. I just need to know where every shipment is, right now, in one place."

---

## Product Implications

**What our product must do to win Sarah:**
1. Cover her top 5 carriers (Maersk, MSC, OOCL, CMA CGM, Evergreen) — non-negotiable
2. Show real-time status without manual data entry
3. Have a free trial she can test with live shipment data

**What would delight Sarah beyond the core job:**
1. Proactive delay alerts via email or notification (she'd never have to check — it tells her)

**What would immediately disqualify our product:**
1. Missing carrier coverage for her top 5 — she won't work around this
2. Requiring IT involvement to set up — she needs to onboard herself
