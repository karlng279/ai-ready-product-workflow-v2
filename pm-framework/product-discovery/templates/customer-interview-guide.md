## Interview Script

### 1. Warm-Up (5 min)

> "Thanks for taking the time today. I want to be upfront — I'm here to learn from you, not to pitch anything. There are no right or wrong answers. I'll be taking notes, but everything is anonymous. Is it okay if I record this for my own reference? [Get consent.]"

> "Can you tell me a bit about your role and what your day-to-day looks like?"

---

### 2. Context Setting — The Job (10 min)

> "I'd like to understand how you [job area — e.g., manage logistics / track shipments / handle team communication]. Can you walk me through the last time you had to do that?"

*(Follow the story, don't interrupt. Ask:)*
- "What triggered that? What made you need to do it then?"
- "Walk me through exactly what you did, step by step."
- "What were you using at the time to get that done?"
- "What was going through your mind at each step?"

---

### 3. Pain & Friction Discovery (10 min)

> "You mentioned [specific thing from their story]. Tell me more about that."

*(Probe for pains:)*
- "What was the most frustrating part of that process?"
- "What did you have to do that felt like it shouldn't be necessary?"
- "Was there a moment where you thought 'there has to be a better way'?"
- "What did you try before settling on [current solution]?"

---

### 4. Switching Story (10 min)

*(If they've changed solutions or behaviors — even if just how they do the job manually:)*

> "You mentioned you used to [prior behavior / tool]. What made you switch?"

*(Probe:)*
- "What was the moment that made you realize you needed to change?"
- "What was the first thing you tried? Why that?"
- "What made you choose [current solution] over the alternatives?"
- "Was there anything that almost stopped you from switching?"

---

### 5. Desired Outcome & Gains (5 min)

> "If this process were perfect — magic wand, anything goes — what would it look like?"

*(Probe:)*
- "What would success look like for you, specifically?"
- "How would you know if the problem was completely solved?"
- "What would you be able to do that you can't do today?"

---

### 6. Wrap-Up (5 min)

> "Is there anything important I didn't ask about that you think I should understand?"

> "Is there anyone else you think I should talk to who has a different perspective on this?"

> "Thank you — this has been really helpful. We'll use what you've shared to make better decisions."
