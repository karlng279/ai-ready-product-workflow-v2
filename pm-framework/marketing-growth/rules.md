# Marketing & Growth — Rules

## What Marketing & Growth Is

Growth answers: How do we acquire, activate, and retain more users? It sits at the intersection of product and marketing — it's not a channel strategy, it's a system of loops that compound over time.

The best growth strategies are embedded in the product itself (Product-Led Growth), not bolted on via ad spend. The goal is to find loops that become self-reinforcing.

---

## Required Components

### 1. Value Proposition
Before growth, you need a clear value proposition — why should a customer choose you over alternatives?

**Value Proposition Canvas (Strategyzer):**
- **Customer Profile:**
  - Jobs: What tasks are customers trying to accomplish?
  - Pains: What frustrations, risks, or obstacles do they face?
  - Gains: What outcomes do they want to achieve?
- **Value Map:**
  - Products & Services: What do you offer?
  - Pain Relievers: How do you reduce or eliminate pains?
  - Gain Creators: How do you create customer gains?
- Fit: Pain relievers and gain creators must map directly to pains and gains.

### 2. Positioning Statement
Formula (Geoffrey Moore):
> "For [target customer] who [has this need or opportunity], [product name] is a [product category] that [key benefit / compelling reason to buy]. Unlike [primary competitive alternative], our product [primary differentiation]."

Rules:
- One statement per target segment.
- "Key benefit" must be the #1 customer-valued outcome, not a feature.
- "Primary differentiation" must be defensible, not aspirational.

### 3. Growth Loop Design
A growth loop is a closed system where output becomes input. More sustainable than a linear funnel.

Types of growth loops:
- **Viral loop:** User action → invites others → new users → repeat. (e.g., Dropbox referral)
- **Content loop:** Users generate content → content attracts new users → new users create more content.
- **Paid loop:** Revenue → ad spend → new customers → more revenue.
- **Product-led loop:** User gets value → shares with team or network → new users → more value.

Document each loop: Entry point → Action → Output → Re-entry condition → Loop metric.

### 4. PLG Flywheel (Product-Led Growth)
Core principle: the product itself is the primary acquisition, activation, and expansion channel.

PLG flywheel stages:
1. **Awareness:** Free tier, viral mechanics, word of mouth
2. **Activation:** Fast time-to-value, self-serve onboarding
3. **Engagement:** Habit-forming usage, notifications, streaks
4. **Expansion:** Usage limits hit → upgrade triggers, team invites
5. **Advocacy:** Power users → referrals, case studies, community

### 5. Retention Mechanics
Retention is the multiplier on all acquisition. Fix retention before scaling.

Key retention levers:
- **Aha moment:** The moment a new user first experiences core value. Identify it, then optimize time-to-aha.
- **Habit formation:** Trigger → Routine → Reward (Nir Eyal's Hook Model). Design for regular return.
- **Notification strategy:** Re-engagement nudges tied to value, not arbitrary. No dark patterns.
- **Expansion revenue:** Current users buying more (upsell, cross-sell). Lower CAC than new acquisition.

## Frameworks to Apply

### Value Proposition Canvas (Strategyzer)
Use for: ensuring your product's value map aligns with what customers actually care about.

### Positioning Statement (Geoffrey Moore)
Use for: crisp, differentiated market positioning. One statement per ICP segment.

### Growth Loop Design
Use for: replacing linear funnel thinking with compounding loop thinking.

### PLG Flywheel
Use for: products that can acquire, activate, and expand without a Sales team as the primary channel.

### Hook Model (Nir Eyal)
Use for: designing habit-forming products.
Components: Trigger (external → internal) → Action (simplest behavior) → Variable Reward → Investment (makes next cycle more likely).

### North Star Alignment
Growth metrics must be tied to the North Star, not vanity metrics. Growth that doesn't move the NSM is waste.

---

## Quality Checklist

Before finalizing a growth artifact:
- [ ] Value Proposition Canvas completed; pains and gains are grounded in customer research
- [ ] Positioning statement names a specific alternative (not "the market")
- [ ] At least one growth loop documented with entry point, action, output, and re-entry condition
- [ ] Aha moment identified with a measurable time-to-aha metric
- [ ] Retention rate measured; growth plan addresses retention before scaling acquisition
- [ ] NSM clearly linked to growth levers

---

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

- **Growth hacking without retention:** Acquiring users into a leaky product burns budget. Fix churn first.
- **Vanity metrics as growth metrics:** Page views, downloads, and sign-ups are top-of-funnel. They don't measure growth.
- **Assuming PLG works for all products:** PLG requires self-serve value. Complex enterprise products often need Sales-Led Growth (SLG) or hybrid.
- **Copying competitor growth tactics:** Growth loops are context-specific. What works for a consumer app may fail for a B2B SaaS.
- **No aha moment definition:** If you don't know when a user "gets it," you can't optimize onboarding.
