## What Positioning Is

Positioning is not a tagline or a marketing slogan. It is an internal strategic tool — a crisp articulation of who you serve, what you do for them, and why you win against the alternative. Good positioning makes all marketing and sales decisions easier because the answer flows from the position.

---

## Positioning Statement (Geoffrey Moore Format)

> **For** [target customer — specific segment, not "everyone"]
>
> **who** [has this specific need or faces this specific problem],
>
> **[product name]** is a **[product category]**
>
> **that** [key benefit — the primary outcome the customer achieves].
>
> **Unlike** [the primary competitive alternative they are currently using],
>
> **our product** [primary differentiator — what we do that the alternative cannot or does not].

## Positioning Components Worksheet

### 1. Target Customer

The specific segment this positioning serves. Not "all businesses" or "everyone who needs X."

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Industry / vertical | [e.g., Freight forwarding, SME segment] |
| Company size | [e.g., 50–500 employees] |
| Role | [e.g., Operations Manager] |
| Behavioral signal | [e.g., Currently tracking shipments in spreadsheets] |

**One-line customer description:** [Who they are in plain language]

### 2. Key Need or Problem

The single most important problem or desire this position addresses.

**Customer job:** [Functional job from persona / VPC]

**Pain being resolved:** [The biggest friction in their current approach]

### 3. Product Category

How customers should mentally categorize the product. This affects what alternatives they consider and what expectations they bring.

**Category:** [e.g., "Shipment visibility platform" vs. "Logistics management suite" — these attract different buyers]

**Why this category frame:** [Rationale]

### 4. Key Benefit

The primary outcome the customer achieves — not a feature, not a capability. A result.

**Key benefit:** [e.g., "Know exactly where every shipment is, without making a single phone call"]

**This is not:** [List what it is NOT — helps avoid feature-creep into the statement]

### 5. Primary Alternative

The one thing customers are currently doing instead of buying your product. Often not a direct competitor.

**Primary alternative:** [e.g., "Manually checking carrier portals and maintaining their own spreadsheet"]

**Why we reference this alternative:** [This is the mental model we're displacing, not necessarily our biggest competitor]

### 6. Primary Differentiator

The one thing we do that the alternative cannot or does not do — and that matters to the target customer.

**Differentiator:** [e.g., "Real-time tracking across all carriers in a single view, with no manual data entry"]

**Is this defensible?** ☐ Yes — because [moat] ☐ Not yet — plan to make it defensible via [action]
